What plants grow best in Birmingham, Alabama?

Birmingham's climate (USDA Zone 7b/8a) supports a wide range of plants. Native perennials like coneflowers, black-eyed Susans, and native azaleas thrive here. Crepe myrtles, hydrangeas, and knockout roses do exceptionally well in Birmingham's heat and humidity. Leaf & Petal's garden experts can recommend specific varieties suited to your yard.

Why are my plants dying in Birmingham's heat?

Alabama's summer heat and humidity can stress plants not suited to the climate. Common causes include overwatering, wrong sun exposure, poor soil drainage, and choosing plants not rated for Zone 7b/8a. Visit Leaf & Petal for expert diagnosis and plant care advice.
